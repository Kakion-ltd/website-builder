Rear Single-Storey Extension

Newbridge, Co. Kildare — A lean-to rear extension creating a bright open-plan kitchen and dining room with sliding glass doors to the garden.

Project Overview

More Room, More Light, More Life

This three-bedroom semi-detached in Newbridge had a compact kitchen that opened onto a small utility area — a layout common in 1990s Irish housing estates that made the ground floor feel cramped and disconnected from the garden. The client's family had grown and they needed more kitchen space, a proper dining area, and better access to the rear garden for their young children.

The project was designed to fall within the exempted development provisions — under 40 m² and within height limits — avoiding the need for planning permission and allowing works to commence quickly. Summerville's proposal was for a lean-to monopitch extension of 34 m² at the rear, with a polycarbonate roof panel section over the new dining area to maximise natural light and a set of slim-profile aluminium sliding doors to the garden.

Design Considerations

The lean-to monopitch roof design was chosen over a flat roof for several practical reasons: lower cost, excellent drainage without specialist waterproofing, and the ability to incorporate a translucent polycarbonate panel section to bring in overhead light without the expense of a bespoke glazed roof lantern. The polycarbonate was specified in a clear opal finish — diffusing direct sunlight to avoid glare while maintaining excellent luminosity.

The existing kitchen was retained in its current position — only the rear wall was removed. The new extension houses a dining table for six and a small soft seating area adjacent to the sliding doors. New kitchen units were fitted along the retained walls, and the overall kitchen-dining space now flows as a single open area.

Scope of Works

  • Demolition of existing rear wall and lean-to porch
  • Strip foundation and concrete ground slab with insulation
  • Block cavity wall construction on three sides
  • Lean-to monopitch roof — fibre cement slates with polycarbonate panel section
  • Aluminium slim-profile sliding doors — 2.4m opening to garden
  • Structural steel lintel over new opening to existing kitchen
  • Underfloor heating — new extension and existing kitchen
  • Polished concrete floor screed, continuous from extension to existing
  • First and second fix electrical throughout
  • New kitchen units and worktop — supply and installation
  • Internal plastering and decoration
  • Patio slab laid outside sliding doors to garden level

The Outcome

The project was completed in 12 weeks. The 34 m² extension more than doubles the effective kitchen-dining area of the ground floor. The polycarbonate roof section provides excellent natural light without overheating, and the sliding doors open to a new poured concrete patio that is level with the internal floor — a detail that greatly improves the connection to the garden.

The client was particularly pleased with the underfloor heating — specified to run from the existing gas boiler with a new zone — which provides comfortable background heat through the slab and makes the extension warm and usable year-round. The project came in on programme and within a fixed-price contract, with no variations.
