Two-Storey Side Extension

Lucan, Co. Dublin — A two-storey side extension adding a ground-floor home office and a fourth bedroom above, matching the existing house finish throughout.

Project Overview

Two Floors of New Space, Seamlessly Added

This three-bedroom semi-detached in Lucan had a generous side return that had never been built on — a 3.1m-wide strip running the full depth of the house. The clients, both working from home, needed a dedicated study on the ground floor and an additional bedroom above as their family expanded. A two-storey side extension was the natural solution.

Planning permission was obtained for the full two-storey structure, and Summerville was appointed as main contractor following a competitive tender. The contract included a fixed price for all works from foundation to finish, including matching the existing K-rend render, roof tiles, and window styles of the original house.

Planning & Design

The planning application was approved subject to conditions — principally that the ground-floor extension be set back 500mm from the front building line and that the materials match the existing house. Summerville's project co-ordinator liaised with the planner throughout the pre-application process to ensure the design was compliant before submission, avoiding any delays.

The structural design required a new reinforced concrete strip foundation alongside the existing house footings, and a series of padstones at first floor level to carry the new beam supporting the bedroom floor. The existing gable wall of the house was used as one leaf of the new cavity wall, minimising disruption to the existing structure.

Scope of Works

  • Planning application preparation and liaison with Kildare County Council
  • Strip foundation alongside existing footings
  • Two-storey block cavity wall construction — 300mm full-fill insulation
  • Concrete first-floor slab and structural steelwork
  • Cut roof to match existing pitch — concrete interlocking roof tiles
  • K-rend silicone render to match existing — colour matched on site
  • New windows — matching existing styles and frames throughout
  • Internal connection through existing gable — new openings with structural lintels
  • Ground floor: dedicated home office with built-in desk and storage
  • First floor: double bedroom with fitted wardrobe
  • Full first and second fix electrical and heating connections
  • Plaster, skim, and decorate throughout
  • Re-laid concrete pathway and new boundary treatment to side

The Outcome

The project was completed over 20 weeks from commencement. The extension adds 68 m² of floor space across two levels and is effectively indistinguishable from the original build — a result the clients were particularly keen on. The colour-matched render and roof tile selection were critical to achieving this, and required careful sampling and sign-off before any external work was applied.

The home office is south-facing and benefits from excellent natural light through the new window. The bedroom above has a Velux window to the rear slope and built-in storage that makes excellent use of the space under the eaves. Compliance documentation under BCAR was issued at practical completion.
