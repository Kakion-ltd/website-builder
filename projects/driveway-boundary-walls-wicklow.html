Driveway & Boundary Walls

Bray, Co. Wicklow — Full driveway replacement in block paving, new rendered boundary walls, and electric gate installation for a detached home.

Project Overview

Kerb Appeal, Properly Done

This detached property in Bray had a tired tarmac driveway, original concrete block boundary walls that were cracked and stained, and a timber gate that had rotted. The client, who had recently renovated the interior of the house, wanted the exterior to match the quality of the inside — a complete overhaul of the front boundary, driveway, and entrance.

Summerville's external works team was engaged to design and deliver the project. The scope included breaking out the existing tarmac, laying a new block paving driveway with a drainage channel, demolishing and rebuilding the front boundary walls in concrete block with a smooth K-rend finish, and installing a pair of powder-coated steel electric swing gates with an intercom system.

Specification & Materials

The client selected a charcoal-and-silver Tobermore Tegula block paving — a tumbled concrete paving block that gives a natural, slightly aged appearance while being extremely durable and low-maintenance. The block was laid in a herringbone pattern with a contrasting border, which adds definition to the driveway and breaks up the large expanse of paving.

The boundary walls were rebuilt in concrete block to 1.2m height, with a brick soldier course on top and a recessed panel detail to add visual interest. The finish is K-rend smooth in off-white — colour-matched to the re-rendered house front which we completed at the same time. The electric swing gates are in powder-coated anthracite steel, with leaf panels to match the wall height and a separate pedestrian wicket gate to the left.

Scope of Works

  • Break-out and disposal of existing tarmac driveway
  • Excavation to formation level and compacted hardcore sub-base (MOT Type 1)
  • Concrete edge restraints and drainage channel at entrance
  • Tobermore Tegula block paving — herringbone with contrasting border
  • Demolition of existing boundary walls
  • New concrete block boundary walls — 1.2m with brick soldier course capping
  • K-rend smooth render to walls — off-white, colour-matched to house
  • Re-render of existing house front — matching finish
  • Electric swing gates — powder-coated anthracite steel with intercom
  • Pedestrian wicket gate to left of main gates
  • Underground ducting for gate motor and electrical supply
  • New coach light fittings to gate piers — LED
  • Clean-down and reinstatement of public footpath

The Outcome

The project was completed in three weeks. The transformation to the front of the property is significant — what was a dated and deteriorating frontage is now a crisp, well-designed entrance that properly reflects the quality of the house behind it. The block paving has excellent drainage — no more puddles at the door — and the electric gates have improved security and convenience for the client.

The client was particularly pleased with the decision to re-render the house front as part of the same project — the consistency of finish across the whole frontage makes the house look as though it has been completely rebuilt, rather than incrementally patched.
