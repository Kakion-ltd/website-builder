Period Home Renovation

Naas, Co. Kildare — A comprehensive renovation of a 1930s semi-detached, tackling damp, rewiring, and a full interior refurbishment from top to bottom.

Project Overview

Restoring Character, Adding Comfort

This 1930s semi-detached in Naas was purchased by the client as a renovation project — the kind of house that had plenty of original character but had been poorly maintained over decades and never properly modernised. The brief was comprehensive: address all structural defects first, then fully rewire and replumb, and finally deliver a high-quality interior fit-out that respected the period character of the home while meeting modern standards of comfort and energy efficiency.

Summerville was engaged from the outset to assess the property alongside the client's architect and a specialist damp surveyor. The assessment identified rising damp in the ground floor walls, failed original plasterwork, a deteriorating timber floor in the main reception room, and an electrical system that was not safe by current standards. A full works programme was agreed before any cosmetic work began.

The Challenge

Period properties in Ireland present a particular set of challenges. The original solid-wall construction means standard cavity wall insulation is not an option — instead, we used internal wall insulation (IWI) on an independent timber batten framework, with mineral wool between and a vapour control layer, finished with plasterboard and skim. This added insulation without compromising the external fabric of the original build.

Rising damp was treated using a combination of chemical damp-proof injection at ground floor level, replacement of the original sand-and-cement plaster with a renovating plaster system, and installation of a new DPC-level air gap membrane in the two most affected rooms. The original timber floor in the front reception was beyond saving and was replaced with engineered oak on a new screed.

Scope of Works

  • Full structural survey and damp investigation prior to works commencing
  • Chemical damp-proof injection at ground floor level throughout
  • Complete strip-out of original plasterwork — all walls and ceilings
  • Internal wall insulation (IWI) — mineral wool on batten, vapour barrier, plasterboard
  • Full rewire — new consumer unit, cabling throughout, LED downlights
  • Full replumb — new copper pipework, pressurised system, cylinder, and radiators
  • New gas boiler (A-rated condensing) with programmer and smart TRVs
  • New sash-style uPVC windows to replace failed original timber frames
  • Engineered oak floor — front reception and hallway
  • New fitted kitchen — supply and installation
  • Two bathrooms and one en-suite — full supply and installation
  • Replacement of all internal doors, skirtings, and architraves
  • Full redecoration throughout — walls, woodwork, and ceilings
  • External render repair and repainting

The Outcome

The project was completed over 18 weeks. What was a tired, damp, and poorly maintained property is now a warm, dry, and beautifully finished family home. The BER rating improved from an F to a C1 — a significant improvement given that solid-wall properties have inherent limitations on achievable ratings without major structural intervention.

The client was particularly satisfied with the quality of the plastering and the care taken to retain original period features — including the original staircase, cast iron fireplace surrounds, and cornicing profiles, which were all carefully protected and restored during the works.
