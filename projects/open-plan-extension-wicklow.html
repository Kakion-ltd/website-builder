Open-Plan Kitchen Extension

Greystones, Co. Wicklow — A rear single-storey extension transforming a dated kitchen into a bright, open-plan living and dining space.

Project Overview

Bringing the Garden In

The clients, a couple with two young children, had lived in their 1980s semi-detached in Greystones for several years before deciding the kitchen needed a complete rethink. The existing layout was compartmentalised and dark — a small galley kitchen with a separate utility room and a dining room that rarely got used. Their vision was an open, social space that connected directly to the rear garden and could function as the true heart of the home.

Planning permission was granted for a rear single-storey extension of 42 m², with a glazed roof lantern as the centrepiece. Summerville was appointed to manage the full build, including demolishing the existing rear wall, structural steelwork, and coordinating the fit-out with the client's kitchen designer.

Design & Structure

The extension sits on reinforced concrete pad foundations with a block cavity wall structure to match the existing house. The flat roof is finished with a warm-roof build-up and a powder-coated aluminium roof lantern — 3m × 1.5m — that floods the space with natural light throughout the day. A set of slim-profile aluminium bifold doors opens the full 4.2m rear elevation to the garden, creating a seamless indoor-outdoor connection in summer months.

The existing kitchen and dining room walls were removed and replaced with a structural steel beam, opening the entire ground floor rear into a single flowing space. The utility room was retained and relocated to a compact area adjacent to the new kitchen run, accessed via a pocket door.

Scope of Works

  • Demolition of existing rear wall and kitchen structures
  • Pad foundations and concrete slab floor with underfloor heating
  • Block cavity wall construction with full-fill insulation
  • Structural steel beam spanning 5.8m to open ground floor rear
  • Warm flat roof with aluminium roof lantern (3m × 1.5m)
  • Aluminium slim-profile bifold doors — 4.2m span, powder-coated anthracite
  • First and second fix electrical — including LED strip lighting and pendants
  • Wet underfloor heating throughout new extension
  • Porcelain large-format floor tiles (1200 × 600mm), laid continuously inside and out
  • Internal plastering, skimming, and decoration throughout
  • Co-ordination and handover to client's kitchen fitter
  • Patio and garden step construction to new floor level

The Outcome

The project was completed in 14 weeks — on programme and within budget. The transformation was significant: what was a fragmented back-of-house area is now a cohesive 62 m² kitchen, dining, and living space that the family use daily. The roof lantern in particular has become a talking point — the clients noted that the space feels warm and bright even on overcast Irish winter days.

The seamless tile level from inside to patio was a detail the clients were particularly keen on, and the bifold doors — when fully open — effectively extend the kitchen into the garden during the warmer months. A completion certificate and compliance documentation were issued at handover.
