Exemptions

Do You Need Planning Permission?

Not all residential works in Ireland require planning permission. Under the Planning and Development Regulations, certain works are considered "exempted development". Here's a general guide:

Usually Exempt

Rear single-storey extensions up to 40m², attic conversions with Velux windows, certain boundary works and garden structures.

May Require Permission

Two-storey extensions, dormer conversions, front or side extensions, and works to listed buildings.

Always Required

New builds on standalone sites, works exceeding exemption thresholds, and any works to protected structures.

We'll clarify your specific situation during our initial consultation. When in doubt, we always recommend confirming exemption status in writing before commencing works.

Timeline

Planning & Design Timeline

Weeks 1–2

Brief & Appointment

Initial consultation, professional appointments confirmed, site survey completed, brief agreed in writing.

Weeks 3–6

Design Development

Architectural drawings prepared, structural calculations completed, pre-planning consultation held if required.

Weeks 7–14

Planning Determination

Application lodged with the local authority. Standard determination period is 8 weeks from valid application date.

Post-Grant

Construction Ready

Full tender pack prepared, contractor appointed, BCAR commencement notice lodged. Build begins.
