Conversion Types

Types of Attic Conversion

The right conversion type depends on your roof structure and the headroom available:

Velux / Rooflight Conversion

The most straightforward option — no change to the roofline. Windows are fitted flush with the existing roof pitch. Often exempt from planning permission.

Dormer Conversion

A box structure added to the rear slope of the roof, creating significantly more headroom and usable floor area. May require planning permission.

Hip-to-Gable Conversion

Extends the hip of the roof to a vertical gable end — ideal for semi-detached properties. Maximises available headroom at the sides of the attic.

Is My Attic Suitable?

As a general guide, you need at least 2.2m of headroom from floor to ridge for a viable conversion. We'll assess your specific roof structure during our free consultation.
